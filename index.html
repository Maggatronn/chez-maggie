Maggie Hughes

Civic technologist, organizer, and product lead with eight years building participatory democracy infrastructure. PhD candidate at MIT Media Lab.

Currently

Co-founder of Convoca — the civic technology organization building V2V and LumoViz.

I build organizing technology — production sociotechnical systems for participatory democracy and co-governance. My practice spans ethnographic research, co-design, and full development lifecycle leadership, with grassroots federations, city planning departments, and research labs.

Advised by Deb Roy at MIT Media Lab, with dissertation committee mentors Eric Gordon and Christopher Le Dantec. Microsoft Sociotechnical Systems PhD Fellow. Metagov 2025 Interoperability Cohort grant. MIT Prize for Open Data, Honorable Mention. Affiliate of the Practicing Democracy Project (Harvard Kennedy School, with Marshall Ganz) and the Data + Feminism Lab (MIT, with Catherine D'Ignazio).

Selected Work

MIT × NYC Dept of City Planning · 2025

Voice to Vision

Production sociotechnical platform deployed with the NYC Department of City Planning. Led the full development lifecycle — from ethnographic research and codesign through deployment — building a living library of community voice that informs neighborhood planning. MIT Prize for Open Data, Honorable Mention.

Paper · Jamaica Plan · White Plains Road

Harvard Kennedy School × Carolina Federation · 2022–present

Evidence-Based Organizing

LumoViz — a relational organizing data platform co-designed, deployed, and iterated with the Carolina Federation over three years. Built production GCP/PostgreSQL infrastructure and accompanying pedagogy for 20+ data practitioners and grassroots organizers across North Carolina.

CSCW 2021

MIT Media Lab · 2024–2025

Conversation Dynamics

Built and evaluated a conversation dynamics visualization tool, developing metrics and a taxonomy for assessing constructive behavior across deliberative conversations.

EMNLP 2025 · DIS 2024 · Demo

Metagov Research Network · 2024–2025

Civic Tech Interoperability

Led the Metagov Interoperability and Data Mesh working group, coordinating 8 civic technologists across global organizations to develop a data ontology and mesh that lets deliberative platforms interoperate in the field.

Aarhus 2025 · Metagov · FAccT 2026 forthcoming

MIT Media Lab × Boston · 2021

Real Talk for Change

Co-led a participatory action research project amplifying community voice in the 2021 Boston Mayoral Election through iterative thematic analysis of 70+ experience-based dialogues with marginalized groups.

Site · Paper

Ars Electronica · 2023

Collective Echos

An embodied spatial audio installation exploring underheard stories.
