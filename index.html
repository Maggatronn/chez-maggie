Maggie Hughes

PhD candidate in Media Arts and Sciences at MIT researching civic technology, participatory design, and constructive communication. I create sociotechnical systems that support meaningful civic engagement and community organizing, combining ethnographic research, co-design practices, and computational methods to amplify community voices in democratic processes.

Voice to Vision

MIT Media Lab × NYC Department of City Planning

A sociotechnical system for transparent civic decision-making that bridges community voices and urban planning processes. Through ethnographic work and co-design with community members and planners, we developed data collection and analysis tools that make neighborhood planning more participatory and accessible.

Voice to Vision: Enhancing Civic Decision-Making (arXiv 2025) • Jamaica Plan Demo • White Plains Road Demo

Evidence-Based Organizing

Harvard Kennedy School × Carolina Federation

Research and design work exploring how community organizers can use data and technology while staying true to their values-driven approach. Through qualitative fieldwork with organizations like Down Home Carolina, we developed interactive tools to visualize organizer networks and relationship-building processes.

From Alinsky to Zoom: Understanding Relational Organizing (CSCW 2021)

Conversation Dynamics

MIT Media Lab × Democracy Next

Computational analysis of constructive communication patterns in civic dialogue. Developed metrics and interactive visualization tools to help facilitators and participants understand conversation dynamics, identify constructive behaviors, and learn from successful dialogue patterns.

Computational Analysis of Conversation Dynamics (EMNLP 2025) • In Pursuit of Constructive Communication (DIS 2024) • Interactive Demo

Civic Tech Interoperability

Metagov Research Network

Leading efforts to create interoperability standards across civic technology platforms. Through collaborative ontology mapping with civic technologists and researchers, we're building connectors between different tools to support more ecosystem-based approaches to democratic participation.

Towards Interoperability: Pursuing an ontology for data exchange (Aarhus 2025)

Keeper

MIT Media Lab × Unity Circles

A synchronous online conversation environment informed by in-person facilitation practices. Ethnographically studied facilitation and community building mechanisms to design a virtual communication platform that scaffolds constructive dialogue in online spaces.

Keeper: A synchronous online conversation environment (CHI 2021) • CSCW 2020

Collective Echos

Ars Electronica 2023 × Spatial Audio Installation

An embodied, spatial audio experience that makes tangible a living library of human experiences. Combining ancient dialogue wisdom with AI-supported technology, this installation creates a pro-social communication space where participants maintain agency over their voice while exploring underheard stories through interactive visualizations and immersive audio.
